Ethical issues

From the perspective of environmental groups, this section discusses the ethical issue of concern for natural organisms when “Quorum Hacker” unintentionally leak into the natural environment. First, it is unlikely that “Quorum Hacker” would unintentionally destroy biofilms in nature. Because the DNA aptamer of the “Quorum Hacker” is designed to act specifically on a particular quormone and is unlikely to inhibit the formation of non-target biofilms. Therefore, the ethical issue for the environment from the “Quorum Hacker” is considered low.

Legal issues

Considering the issues of “Quorum Hacker” on biodiversity from a political and legal perspective. Because “Quorum Hacker” use DNA as robotic parts, they do not have specific genetic information. Also, since they do not have metabolic and self-replication mechanisms, which are elements of living organisms, they cannot be considered biotechnologically modified organisms as stipulated by the Cartagena Act. Therefore, it is highly unlikely that “Quorum Hacker” will have an impact on biodiversity, and legal issues are unlikely to be a problem.

Social issues

We discuss the potential societal concerns that "Quorum Hacker" may cause from the viewpoint of biofilm utilizers. The idea of using biofilms to recover resources such as methane gas from sewage currently exists, but it is unlikely that "Quorum Hacker" would inhibit that project. This is because the "Quorum Hacker" is expected to be used in areas where biofilm formation is undesirable, such as plant piping at power plants, and therefore, it is expected to be able to live separately from plants that utilize biofilms. Therefore, there should be no major social concerns with the implementation of "Quorum Hacker".
